What “AI Readiness” actually means

AI Readiness isn't about whether your team uses ChatGPT. It's about whether the work the organization actually does is set up to benefit from AI – so it saves your team time, raises the quality of what gets produced, and gives senior leaders back the hours for the strategic, mission, and people work that only they can do.

Five conditions matter:

  • Your data is usable. AI can't work with information that lives only in someone's head.
  • You know where your time is going. Repetitive tasks you can name are tasks you can delegate.
  • You know your constituents. Who they are, what they need, and how they reach you.
  • There's a clear way you do things. AI amplifies what's repeatable. If your methods live in one person's head, there's nothing to amplify yet.
  • You have momentum. You've tried things, you're curious, and you want to move.

Where the leverage is

The opportunity areas

Four areas where the diagnostic surfaced real leverage – each tagged with its source. These are directional, not finished specs; the AI Plan is where they get prioritized and scoped into actual builds.

1

Executive Coordination Workflow

Anchor opportunity · Speaks directly to the magic wand and the named headache
AI Readiness Check

The direct answer to scheduling the CEO's calendar and to “scheduling and follow-up reminders.” A structured workflow for the executive coordination work running through the Chief of Staff seat: a single intake point for meeting and decision requests, a drafting layer for follow-up communications, an automated reminder cadence for items that need to come back, and a weekly briefing format that pulls together board, funder, and senior-team commitments. The goal is to return Chief of Staff time to the work only Nicci can do – cross-program judgment, CEO interface, and strategic project support – rather than reactive coordination. None of this touches resident or donor data.

2

Constituent Inquiry Triage System

Time-leverage opportunity · Closes the next-day response gap
AI Readiness Check Public research

Response time at “next day or later” is the weakest single answer in the diagnostic. Public research fills in why it shows up that way: North Shore CDC fields inquiries from prospective and current residents, applicants, donors, board members, partner organizations, YouthBuild candidates, Small Business clients, and the general public – with no single team owning the inbound queue. This area is a triage layer that routes incoming asks to the right program owner, drafts first-touch responses for the recurring patterns, and surfaces which inquiries are stuck. Property-level resident requests stay with Peabody Properties under contract – this system covers the organizational inbox and program-level inquiries that currently land in shared or individual staff email.

3

Operating Knowledge Capture

Risk-reduction opportunity · Closes the SOP and key-person-risk gap
AI Readiness Check

SOPs partial and outdated. Key-person risk at the top of the scale. That pairing is the most consequential signal in the diagnostic for an organization of this size and complexity. This area is a structured capture process for the operating knowledge currently running on shared memory: how each program's enrollment and onboarding actually works, who owns which external relationships, what the recurring annual calendar looks like for each program, and where the handoffs between programs happen. The output is not a binder – it's a maintainable set of program-level operating documents. YouthBuild is likely the right starting point because its external accreditation drivers and public-facing structure make the existing process more accessible to document first.

4

Constituent Data Reconciliation

Acceleration opportunity · Activates the 80% Data & Systems score
AI Readiness Check

Data is held across five systems. The same person – a resident who was a YouthBuild alumnus and is now a donor household, or a partner organization whose staff include current residents – likely exists in more than one system with no link between them. This area is a reconciliation layer that identifies the cross-system overlaps, surfaces where the same constituent is tracked separately, and supports a single decision-ready view at the moments when that view matters most: annual reporting, grant applications, board packets, and cross-program enrollment decisions. This is the area that activates the data score – the systems are already in place, the foundation is already in place; the work is the structural one of seeing across them.
